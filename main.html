Man of the Soil

Low-relief (45 × 45 cm). This piece imagines Demas rising from the ground that taught him. It is a simple, essential portrait: creativity emerging from soil, tradition and everyday craft.

The work echoes our conversations and the phrase that guided the project: “If he has recognition out there, it’s because of what he did in here.”

Our Story

An exploration of vernacular as a language

We met Baba in a quick studio session and heard a simple, disarming line: “If it is not vernacular, what else is there?” That sentence folded the room: vernacular was not a style to be assigned; it was a language you learned from home, a way to know a client and a place.

“Vernacular is the natural language called design”

From the Zaria Art Society and the idea of Natural Synthesis, through projects such as the Dominican Mission Chapel and the New Culture Studio, Demas showed us that form must grow from context. His buildings are not ornaments; they are answers — responses to climate, ritual, craft, and the ways people live.

This page is not a biography. It is a short letter — an exploration — that seeks the heartbeat in material, not in labels.
