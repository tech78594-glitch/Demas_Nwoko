“Vernacular is the natural language called design” [cite: 153]

We met Baba in a quick studio session and heard a simple, disarming line: “If it is not vernacular, what else is there?” [cite: 144] That sentence folded the room: vernacular was not a style to be assigned; it was a language you learned from home, a way to know a client and a place[cite: 145, 146].

From the Zaria Art Society to the New Culture Studio, Demas taught us that design should begin with listening — to materials, ritual, climate and the way a people live[cite: 148]. He practiced Natural Synthesis: merging indigenous ideas with modern tools so that form and meaning stayed native and useful[cite: 149].

Produced by The Legacy Initiative in partnership with Arc Bofu. This project is a short letter — an exploration — that seeks the heartbeat in material, not in labels[cite: 152, 161].
